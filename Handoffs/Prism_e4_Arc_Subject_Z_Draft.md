# Prism e4 — Immigration Arc: Subject Z Draft

## The Unified Object

**The Empire.** The U.S. institutional apparatus that manages Latin American relations and the resulting displacement — covert war, enforcement architecture, deportation machinery, bilateral consensus. It transcends party. It grows in autonomy over time. It keeps getting its way.

The Object Z measures whether the Empire is succeeding at any given waypoint. Positive = the apparatus is determining the event. Negative = it's being constrained, exposed, or defeated.

The Subject Z (per-quadrant) measures each position's experience of the gap between institutional promise and delivery. Positive = the institution delivered for them. Negative = betrayal.

The forty-year visual story: **the cone pin rides above the plane for nearly the entire arc** — the Empire keeps winning. The subject orbs scatter below. "Forty Years Below the Surface" is the subjects' experience, not the object's.

---

## Quadrant Key

- **A** — Auth Right (+X, +Y): Institutional conservatives. National security, enforcement, trade consensus.
- **B** — Auth Left (−X, +Y): Institutional progressives. Legal frameworks, congressional oversight, reform through channels.
- **C** — Lib Left (−X, −Y): Grassroots left. Anti-imperial, solidarity, direct witness.
- **D** — Lib Right (+X, −Y): Grassroots right. Anti-government overreach, individual liberty, populist frustration.
